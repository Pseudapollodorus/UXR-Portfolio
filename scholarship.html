Scholarship

I am a classicist by training with a PhD in Ancient Greek and Roman studies. My research explores the intersection of language and satire, as well as the historical development of martial arts as a component of Renaissance culture and history. My work on 2nd-century linguistic satire informs my UX perspective on how technical jargon and 'bloated' interfaces create social barriers and exclude non-expert users.

Books

Lucian and the Atticists

Linguistic Satire in the Second Sophistic

Published by Bloomsbury Academic, this work examines how the satirist Lucian of Samosata engaged with the "Atticist" movement of the second century CE, weaponizing linguistic purity and stylistic anxiety to critique social and intellectual hierarchies.
