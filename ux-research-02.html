User Experience Research

Applying rigorous, evidence-based methodologies to solve complex product challenges. These projects represent collaborative research sprints focusing on healthcare accessibility, financial usability, and behavioral retention.

Healthcare / RPM

Maternal Care Access

  • • Client: a hypothetical health-tech startup named "Kairos".
  • • Problem: Maternal care deserts in rural Arkansas and the Mississippi Delta face a "wait-time crisis" due to physician shortages and lack of broadband/smartphone access.
  • • Approach: Conducted market research and SWOT analysis. My role was to compare features of competing products and services to identify market segments experiencing serious need and currently being underserved by existing offerings.
  • • Results: Projected a 36% increase in patient adherence and a 400%+ ROI for clinics by preventing high-cost emergency air transports.
  • • Impact: Our findings shifted the product from a high-bandwidth app to a "Virtual OB Unit" for general clinics, demonstrating a 400%+ ROI through emergency transport avoidance.

Fintech / Usability

Financial Goal Tracker Usability

  • • Client: SaveStack, a (fictional) personal finance app designed for tracking income, expenses, and savings.
  • • Problem: Early analytics showed low engagement and incomplete goals for a newly launched "Goal Tracker" feature, as users found the interface confusing and difficult to navigate.
  • • Approach: Developed four distinct user personas to represent the customer base; planned and conducted task-based usability testing using a mobile wireframe prototype; measured quantitative performance (task time, completion rates) and qualitative satisfaction (Likert scales).
  • • Results: The feature received a critically low average flexibility rating and multi-goal visibility scores, indicating users could not see how different goals impacted one another; core tasks were either failed or abandoned by participants due to high friction.
  • • Impact: Identified that "Onboarding Overload" was a primary cause of user abandonment; proposed a V2 Roadmap featuring a "Progressive Setup" to reduce user friction and a "Flexible Contribution Model" to account for irregular incomes.

E-Commerce / Quantitative

Food Delivery Customer Retention

  • • Client: A food delivery platform, "TableGram," with strong acquisition but poor long-term retention.
  • • Problem: High prevalence of "Promotional Nomads," users who switch between apps for discounts, with 65% of users reporting they would not be disappointed if the service disappeared tomorrow.
  • • Approach: Designed and simulated a mobile-optimized survey to identify the "Competitive Gap" and primary drivers of app selection; proposed and simulated A/B test of a gamified "Streak Bonus" to evaluate impact on order frequency; performed quantitative analysis including an independent samples t-test and Cohen's $d$ effect size calculation.
  • • Results: The Streak Bonus feature drove a 60% increase in average order frequency (from ~3.15 to ~5.05 orders over 6 weeks); high statistical significance ($p < .001$) with a large practical effect size (Cohen's $d = 1.11$); 95% Confidence Interval suggests a true effect of 1.56 to 2.24 additional orders per user.
  • • Impact: Our research indicated that gamification could move users past the psychological threshold where the app becomes a habit rather than a utility; recommended a full 100% rollout of the Streak Bonus feature to maximize customer lifetime value.
